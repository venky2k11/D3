Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by a newly discovered coronavirus. The virus that causes COVID-19 is mainly transmitted through droplets generated when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or exhales. These droplets are too heavy to hang in the air, and quickly fall on floors or surfaces. One can be infected by breathing in the virus if we are within close proximity of someone who has COVID-19, or by touching a contaminated surface and then our eyes, nose or mouth. With this, the general expectation is that places with highest density of human population should have large impact of COVID-19. Below chart's look at the spread of COVID-19 from a density perspective and the countries that have been impacted the most.

Laboratory Confirmed cases - by Country:


World map view showing total laboratory confirmed cases (till 4th July 2020) grouped by population density. Circle size reflects the total confirmed cases. For a pandemic like Corona virus, we would expect that highest density areas are likely be worst effected. However, looking at the chart, we see that this is not true. Countries like South Korea and Singapore have done far better in controlling the spread when compared to lower density countries like US and South America. Looking at the above map view, we could see that countries like US, Brazil, Russia and India have very high numbers compared to other countries. Hovering over the circle, we could see that US has the highest number of confirmed cases i.e. 2839436. Let's look at the next chart to find out Top 10 effected countries.

Top 10 Countries (Confirmed/Death cases):


Top 4 countries shown here in this chart for number of confirmed cases is what we expected by looking at the above world map chart. The next 6 countries that have very close numbers to each others. US numbers are close to double of what next highest country Brazil.

By selecting the button "Death cases", we see that US and Brazil have again appeared at the top of the chart with US almost double the numbers. Countries that appeared in top 10 for confirmed cases also make it to the top 10 list for death cases with exception of France and Iran suggesting, the death rates in France and Iran could be very high compared to other countries. Russia seems to have better control over death rate as even though they had high confirmed numbers, their death toll is comparitively lower than other top countries. From this, we could say that higher confirmed cases could lead to higher death toll however with greater control measures, we could reduce the death rates.
